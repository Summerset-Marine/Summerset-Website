Permanent Piers on the Oconomowoc Chain: A Guide

The lakes of the Oconomowoc / Lake Country area are among the most active waterfront markets Summerset Marine Construction serves. Okauchee, Lac La Belle, Nagawicka, and Beaver Lake each have their own character — different depths, different bottom conditions, different wave exposure — and the right waterfront system depends on understanding the specific lake where your property sits.

A Connected System of Distinct Lakes

Okauchee Lake connects to the broader chain, which creates some current and wave exposure that seasonal systems struggle with over time. Lac La Belle is more sheltered and clearer, with conditions that are ideal for permanent installation. Nagawicka Lake's size creates open-water exposure on its north and west shores that favors the structural stability of permanent systems. Beaver Lake, with its average depth of 15 feet and protected position, is an excellent candidate for Lifetime permanent pier systems at any budget level.

The Hidden Cost of Seasonal Systems

Many Lake Country homeowners have used seasonal piers for years without calculating the true annual cost: installation labor, removal labor, storage fees, hardware replacement, and the time lost at each end of the season. When you spread a permanent system's cost over 25 to 30 years of ownership — and factor in zero annual maintenance costs — the economics consistently favor permanent for property owners who plan to stay.
